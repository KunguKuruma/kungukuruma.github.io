01

Research Direction

what I want to work on

I am keen on developing AI models that improve disease diagnostics, and more specifically cancer detection, to alleviate diagnostic inaccuracies and reduce the fatalities they cause. This is work that is universally needed, wherever patients and clinicians depend on accurate, timely diagnosis.

The fundamental problem is not the accuracy of these systems in the lab, but their ability to hold up where they are needed most. I therefore aim to build systems that are robust across hospitals and scanners, explainable to the clinicians who depend on them, and designed for real infrastructure and data realities rather than idealised ones. Furthermore, I intend to develop multimodal architectures that integrate medical imaging and patient history, evaluated on specificity and false positive reduction rather than accuracy alone. The goal is detection that changes outcomes, not benchmarks.

Independent Research

A PhD level research proposal addressing the gap between high performing experimental lung cancer AI models and real world clinical deployment within the Irish healthcare system.

  • Proposed an end to end detection system spanning GDPR compliant data ingestion, a structured database layer, standardised CT/PET-CT preprocessing, deep learning classification, embedded explainability, and a clinician facing dashboard.
